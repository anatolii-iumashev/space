# MARKETING.md

Marketing configuration for Space — unified workspace with Drive, Mail & Calendar.

## Product
Space is a self-hosted workspace that combines calendar, file management, and email into a single integrated platform. 

It syncs with existing services (e.g. Google Calendar, Gmail) via standard protocols (CalDAV, IMAP/SMTP) and stores files in S3-compatible storage or local filesystem. The goal is to reduce app switching and give users control over their data and files.


## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

- Small teams and solopreneurs who want a unified workspace
- Individual professionals and freelancers
- Power users frustrated by app switching
- Tech-savvy, comfortable with self-hosted solutions
- Value privacy and data ownership

## Brand Voice

- Clear, direct, concise
- Technical but accessible
- Avoid buzzwords and marketing fluff

## Tone

- Practical > inspirational
- Feature-focused, not hype-driven
- Honest about limitations and roadmap
- Use real examples over abstract promises

## Messaging Pillars

- **One workspace, everything in place** — calendar, files, email, unified
- **Your data, your rules** — self-hosted, no vendor lock-in
- **Simple by default** — no bloat, no unnecessary features
- **Open and extensible** — built with open standards (CalDAV, IMAP, S3)
- Your space, free and open. Includes: mail, calendar, drive
- Free. Open. Yours. Mail, calendar, drive
- Your free space. Open source. Mail, calendar, drive
- Space: your free, open workspace. Mail, calendar, drive
- Space: drive, mail, calendar. Free. Open. Yours.

## Positioning

Space is a self-hosted workspace that replaces multiple SaaS tools with a single application. Not a competitor to Google Workspace — a focused alternative for individuals who want control over their data.

## Channels

- Primary: GitHub README, dev.to, Hacker News
- Secondary: Twitter/X, Reddit (r/selfhosted, r/webdev)
- Content: Technical blog posts, launch announcements, integration guides

## Campaign Rules

- Always link to the GitHub repository
- Include clear setup instructions
- Mention self-hosted nature prominently
- No exaggerated claims ("best", "revolutionary", etc.)
